# PR-14 — Residual correctness and bounded retries

## Status and outcome

Implementation and local verification are complete; independent rereview is pending. PR-14 applies one GET/HEAD-only retry primitive to FRED, ALFRED, Yahoo, Stooq, and live FRED fallback requests. Only transport failures, HTTP 429, and HTTP 5xx retry. Caller aborts, other 4xx responses, and successful-HTTP parse/validation failures do not retry.

Every attempt has an independent hard timeout (10 seconds by default, capped at 30 seconds), its own `AbortController`, and deterministic timeout provenance. The production backoff defaults to three attempts, 250 ms base, 2-second delay cap, and full jitter; callers cannot exceed five attempts or a 30-second configured delay cap. A cold `loadLive` shares one atomic 32-attempt budget across its concurrent price and stress loads. Budget exhaustion fails closed as `ATTEMPT_BUDGET_EXHAUSTED`.

Retryable intermediate bodies and terminal non-OK bodies discarded by FRED/provider callers are canceled best-effort; cancellation failures never replace the original outcome. Terminal responses returned by the retry primitive itself remain readable.

Champion formulas, weights, freshness rules, 45/55 verdict bands, hysteresis, stress thresholds, portfolio policy, and existing walk-forward/factor-research diagnostic values are unchanged. There is no migration.

## Known limitations

- Retry policy does not interpret `Retry-After`; it uses bounded local full-jitter backoff.
- The 32-attempt budget is scoped to one `loadLive` invocation, not a distributed/global quota.
- Existing `LEGACY_9_SIGNAL_DIAGNOSTIC` research numbers still include the zero-weight `vol` diagnostic by design; PR-17 owns a preregistered eight-factor recomputation.
- Staging and production behavior remain unverified because no deployment or remote access was authorized.
